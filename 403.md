## Topic 4: Survival Analysis Fundamentals
* What do you mean by lifetime and lifetime distribution? Define probability density function $f(t)$, survival function $S(t)$, hazard function $h(t)$, and cumulative hazard function. Establish the interrelationships among these functions. [2024(4a), 2022(1a), 2020(1a), 2019(3)]
* What are the goals and purposes of survival analysis? [2024(3a), 2020(1a), 2019(3)]
* Explain with examples: increasing failure rate (IFR), decreasing failure rate (DFR), and constant failure rate (CFR). [2020(1b), 2019(5)]
* Derive the expressions for probability density function, survival function, and hazard function given specific baseline functions (e.g., $F(t) = 1-\exp[-((t-\mu)/\alpha)^2]$ or $h(t) = \frac{\lambda pt^{p-1}}{1+\lambda t^p}$). [2024(4b), 2022(1b), 2019(18, 23)]
* Find the failure rate of the failure time distribution specified by the survivor function $S(t) = \exp[-\frac{\beta}{\lambda}t^{\beta-1}]$. [2023(3a), 2022(2b), 2021(2b)]

* Define mean lifetime and mean residual life (MRL). Obtain the mean lifetime and MRL of a specific distribution (e.g., Weibull), and prove specific MRL identities such as $m(t) = \frac{1}{S(t)}\int_t^\infty S(x)dx$. [2022(5a, 5b), 2020(3c), 2019(7)]
* Show that $S(t) = \frac{r(0)}{r(t)} e^{-\int \frac{1}{r(y)} dy}$. [2023(2a), 2020(3c)]
