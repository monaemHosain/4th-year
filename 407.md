### Topic 1: Probability Proportional to Size (PPS) Sampling

* Discuss the cumulative total method of selecting a PPS sample of size $n$, including its steps, merits, and drawbacks. [2024(1a), 2022(1c), 2020(1a)]

* Show that, with Lahiri's method of PPS with replacement sampling, the probability for the $i$-th population unit to get selected on the first effective draw is proportional to the size of the $i$-th unit of the auxiliary variable. [2023(3b)] 
* State the unbiased estimator of the population total or mean under PPS sampling with replacement, derive its standard error/variance, and find/compare the gain in efficiency due to PPS sampling with that of simple random sampling (SRS). [2024(1b), 2022(2a), 2021(1c), 2020(1b), 2019(1b)] 
* Is PPS sampling always more efficient than equal probability sampling? [2023(4a)] 
* Discuss the procedure of drawing a PPS sample of $n$ villages by linear systematic sampling technique. [2022(1b)] 
* Discuss why it is more difficult to select a varying probabilities without replacement (WOR) sample compared to a with replacement sample. * Explain the terms first and second-order inclusion probabilities, and describe how to calculate inclusion probabilities in different sampling techniques (e.g., simple random and stratified sampling). [2022(2b), 2021(2a), 2020(2a), 2019(2a)]
